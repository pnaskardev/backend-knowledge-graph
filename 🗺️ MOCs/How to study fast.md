# ⚡ How to study fast (interview mode)

> Goal: breadth + recall, not deep mastery. Fill a note in ~5 min, review in ~30 sec.

## The loop
1. Open a note. **Search the topic yourself** (docs, a video, an article) — don't wait for perfect understanding.
2. Fill only four things: **🎯 Definition** (one line), **💡 Why**, **⚙️ How** (2–3 bullets), **⚖️ Trade-off**. Stop there.
3. Write **one or two 🃏 Recall cards** in `Q → A` form. This is the part that makes it stick.
4. Move on. Don't polish.

## Order (fastest useful path)
Cover **definitions across a whole domain first**, then deepen. Breadth beats depth for interviews.
1. [[Distributed Systems MOC]] — CAP, consistency, idempotency, retries, circuit breaker (highest ROI)
2. [[Databases MOC]] — ACID/BASE, indexing, replication, sharding, isolation
3. [[Caching MOC]] — patterns + the 4 problems
4. [[Messaging MOC]] — queues, Kafka vs RabbitMQ, ordering
5. [[Microservices MOC]] — SAGA, CQRS, the resilience patterns
6. [[Kubernetes MOC]] — just the core objects
7. [[System Design MOC]] — practice out loud, this is where it all combines
8. [[Low Level Design MOC]] — SOLID + 2–3 problems if time

## Reviewing (do this daily)
- Open a note, **cover the right side of each 🃏 card**, answer from memory, check.
- Got it wrong? Move that card to a "revisit" list and see it again tomorrow.
- Once a card is easy 2 days running, you're done with it.

## The interview test
You "know" a System Design note when you can say its **🔑 2–3 things** out loud and name the **⚖️ trade-off** without looking. That's the bar — not a perfect essay.

## Don't
- Don't write paragraphs. Bullets only.
- Don't chase every link mid-note — note the gap, keep moving, fill it later.
- Don't aim for complete. Aim for *recallable*.
